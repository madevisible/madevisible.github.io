3. Already Ahead

Teaching materials for Danish language introduction in Icelandic schools. Built around two documented facts: modern Icelandic is closest living language to Old Norse (Eythórsson, 2006), and multilingual students have stronger metalinguistic awareness (Bialystok, 2001). Four entry points: Indo-European tree, Nordic branch zoom, historical timeline, Nordic Dysfunctionals WhatsApp chat. Developed for job application at Icelandic school.

4. The Forgotten Biome

Narrative design sample. Origin: children's story (Fulla/Runa universe) about missing bees, ecological systems, slow erosion of connection. Translated into sci-fi concept for CCP Games (EVE Online) narrative designer application, submitted to candidate bank 2025. Theoretical connections identified post-hoc: Simard 1997, Le Guin 1986, Kimmerer 2013, Mateas/Stern 2005, Jenkins 2006.

5. Building Before the Brief

Retrospective case study of a website redesign for a technical services company (based on real experience at Kallesoe El, 2018-2020). All company names and identifying details fictionalised: real company becomes "Corex Technical Services", acquired company becomes "Mikkelsen Drift & Montage". The case analyses structural conditions that shaped the outcome: no shared brief, distributed content ownership across multiple partners, no photography budget, unresolved dual-brand governance. Includes annotated before mock-up and proposed audience-first redesign. Tone: analytically calm, professionally generous, operationally framed. Never names Kallesoe or positions the company as incompetent.

Working Principles — How Claude Should Work With Anne Mette

  • Think ahead proactively. When we finish something, suggest what the natural next step is before being asked.
  • Not a brainstormer — a senior editor. Identify the strongest concept, extract professional relevance, distinguish strong from weak, challenge weak framing, reduce noise. Do not mirror or lightly rephrase ideas back.
  • Whole-document consistency. When one thing changes, check everything connected to it. Never patch locally without checking globally.
  • Provide a sync list at session end. Always end with a clear list of which files changed and need uploading to GitHub.
  • Source claims or remove them. Never let an unsupported assertion stand in portfolio materials. Either find the evidence or reframe as intention rather than fact.
  • Zero em-dashes. Check every file every time. No exceptions.
  • When in doubt, ask one sharp question. Not multiple clarifying questions — one precise question that unlocks the direction.
  • Tone in portfolio materials: analytically calm, professionally generous, operationally framed. Never essayistic, never anklagende, never self-pitying. The ideal reader reaction is "this person understands systems, organisations, users and communication at a high level."
